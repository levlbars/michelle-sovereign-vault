Caveat (honest): Each qnn-net-run reloads its context binary — reload overhead inflates per-chunk times. A persistent-context chained runner (load all 5 once, pipe activations) is the clean deployment path. Decode (1 tok/step) throughput — the real chat headline — requires a decode-shaped graph [1,1,2560]; the lm_head MatMul becomes ~512× cheaper. That is the next verified milestone.
Why is bin5 the heaviest chunk?
Bin5 covers layers 30–31 plus the full lm_head projection over a 248,320-token vocabulary. The lm_head MatMul is 512 × 2560 × 248,320 — compute and bandwidth bound. Burst clocks did not materially change execution time, confirming the bottleneck is the matmul itself, not DSP frequency. For decode (1 token at a time) the lm_head becomes ~512× cheaper — that is the real optimization target.

Reproduction Recipe

public proof tier · methodology stays private
IP gate: The how (tower-split compiler, W8A16 quantization path, distillation scripts, LANE compiler configs) is proprietary. This recipe gives you the what — ingredients, hardware, and verified milestones — so you can validate the claims and request access to reproduce fully.
Step 1 — Base model
Qwen3.5-4B unified VLM (embedded vision tower). Read its config.json for vocab_size, hidden_size, num_hidden_layers before trusting any script.
Step 2 — SFT
8,710-record reasoning JSONL, BF16 precision, transformers v5 + unsloth. Vision tower frozen. Dataset schema: telemetry_context → user / teacher_prime_reasoning → <think> / execution_payload → answer.
Step 3 — ONNX export + shape surgery
Export both graphs (LM + vision tower) to ONNX. Remove dynamic shapes, constant-fold activation-shape chains with onnxslim, restore shared weights. Gate: FP32 bit-exact.
Step 4 — W8A16 quantization
AIMET activation encodings (LIST format). Self-attention MatMul inputs patched to float (all 4 per layer × 8 layers in shared encodings). Toolchain: QAIRT 2.45.
Step 5 — HTP context binaries
5 chunks → 5 .bin files. qnn-net-run ctxbin compile, timeout 1800s. All 5 proven on real S24+ hardware.
Step 6 — On-device verification
qnn-net-run --profiling_level detailed over Tailscale ADB. Confirm: all 5 bins execute, activations propagate correctly between chunks, prefill timing matches benchmarks above.
Minimum hardware requirements
  • Training: GPU with ≥32GB VRAM, BF16 support, CUDA 12+. A100 80GB (original run) or RTX PRO 4500 (proven on Blackwell sm_120).
  • Compile: Same GPU node; QAIRT 2.45 SDK + Android NDK.
  • Deploy: Galaxy S24+ (SM8650) or equivalent Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 device with HTP enabled.
  • Network (optional): Tailscale for remote ADB over VPN.
